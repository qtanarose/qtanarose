**Behavioral Economics | Why do people act that way?**

- Studies the **"hidden whys"** behind human choices.
- why people are often **irrational** (making choices based off feelings, that don't always make logical sense).
- Used in business and marketing to predict how people *actually* behave.
1. **What drives our decisions?**
    - It's not just logic; it's often "irrational" feelings or habits.
    - People don't always do what is best for them, even when they know better.
2. **How do we predict behavior?**
    - Actual vs. Expected; Instead of looking at how people *should* act, it looks at how they *actually* act.
    - Focuses on the gap between what our brain thinks is right (like saving money or eating healthy) and what we actually do.
3. **Why does it matter?**
    - Helps us understand why people make mistakes with money, time, or health.
    - Used in business to predict human behavior and design things that fit how human brains really work.
- **Bounded Rationality:** Your brain has **limits** (not enough time, info, or brainpower), so you settle for "good enough" instead of "perfect".
- **Prospect Theory:** We are terrified of losing. A **loss** usually hurts about **twice as much** as a gain feels good.
- **Nudges:** Tiny changes in how choices are presented can "nudge" you to a certain decision.
